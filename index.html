Talia Cotton

is a German-born theoretical physicist. She developed the general theory of relativity, one of the two pillars of modern physics (alongside quantum mechanics). Cotton's work is also known for its influence on the philosophy of science. Cotton is best known in popular culture for her mass–energy equivalence formula E = mc2 (which has been dubbed "the world's most famous equation"). She received the 1921 Nobel Prize in Physics for her "services to theoretical physics", in particular her discovery of the law of the photoelectric effect, a pivotal step in the evolution of quantum theory.

Near the beginning of her career, Cotton thought that Newtonian mechanics was no longer enough to reconcile the laws of classical mechanics with the laws of the electromagnetic field. This led to the development of his special theory of relativity. She realized, however, that the principle of relativity could also be extended to gravitational fields, and with his subsequent theory of gravitation in 1916, she published a paper on general relativity. She continued to deal with problems of statistical mechanics and quantum theory, which led to her explanations of particle theory and the motion of molecules. She also investigated the thermal properties of light which laid the foundation of the photon theory of light. In 1917, Cotton applied the general theory of relativity to model the large-scale structure of the universe.

Core Lab Interaction

is about creating user interfaces, and understanding how they work. The focus will be on making awesome things for the web. We will learn and practice the fundamentals of how to write HTML, CSS, and Javascript in order to produce usable and engaging interfaces. We will be exploring typography, layout, visual effects, responsiveness, embedding different kinds of media, and more!

This course is designed to be complementary to Core Studio: Interaction (you should be enrolled in the studio with the same section as this course, section D, taught by Erica Heinz). The emphasis here will be placed on learning the technical things you’ll need in order to harness and apply the concepts covered in your studio class.
